The Narrows

The Narrows — Hiking through the Virgin River — Photo: NPS
💧
Always check river flow before going. Below 50 CFS = easy. 50–70 CFS = moderate. 70–150 CFS = challenging. Above 150 CFS = CLOSED.   Check Live Flow →

Bottom-Up (No Permit)

Start at Temple of Sinawava (shuttle stop 9). Walk the 1-mile Riverside Walk, then enter the river. Go as far as you want:

  • Wall Street (~1.5 mi in) — narrowest, most dramatic section
  • Orderville Canyon (~2 mi in) — popular turnaround, 5 mi RT total
  • Big Spring (~5 mi in) — farthest point without permit, 10 mi RT

Time: 2–8 hours depending on distance.

Top-Down Through-Hike (Permit Required)

16 miles from Chamberlain's Ranch to Temple of Sinawava. Mostly downhill in the river.

  • Apply via recreation.gov lottery — 2 days before, midnight–3 PM MT
  • Requires private shuttle to Chamberlain's Ranch (outfitters offer this)
  • Day hike: 10–14 hours. Overnight: 12 campsites available

Gear Rental (~$25–32/day)

Essentials (Always Rent):
  • Canyoneering boots — rocks are like "greased bowling balls"
  • Neoprene socks — water ranges 38–65°F depending on season
  • Sturdy walking stick (NOT collapsible — they fail in fast water)
Cold Season Add-Ons:
  • Dry bibs — waterproof with latex ankle gaskets (spring/fall)
  • Full dry suit — Kokotat with wrist/ankle gaskets (winter)
Outfitters:
  • Zion Outfitter — near south entrance
  • Zion Adventures — 36 Lion Blvd, Springdale
  • Zion Guru — 1013 Zion Park Blvd
☠️
Cyanobacteria Warning: Toxic cyanobacteria detected in the Virgin River. NEVER drink river water — no filter can remove the toxins. Get drinking water from side springs only, then filter/disinfect. A dog has died from exposure.

Angels Landing

Angels Landing — Chain section with 1,000ft drops — Photo: NPS

The Hike

  • 5.4 mi RT, 1,488ft gain, ~4 hours
  • Start: The Grotto (shuttle stop 6)
  • First 2 mi: switchback trail through Refrigerator Canyon
  • Walter's Wiggles: 21 tight switchbacks to Scout Lookout
  • Scout Lookout: Excellent views, no permit needed here
  • Final 0.5 mi: Narrow spine, chains, 1,000ft+ drops both sides
  • Bring gloves for chain section (better grip)
  • Do NOT hike if wet or icy — sandstone becomes extremely slippery
  • Trekking poles NOT recommended on chain section

Permit System (2026)

Required for any portion past Scout Lookout. $6 application + $3/person if selected. Max group: 6.

Insider Tips

From Reddit & YouTube
1
Arrive before 8 AM or skip the car entirely. Park lots fill by 8:00–8:30 AM. Park in Springdale and take the free town shuttle, or rent e-bikes to bypass lines completely.
2
Visit late October for ideal conditions. "The fall colors are peaking, temps are in the 70s, and the summer families have long since gone home." Alternatively, winter for solitude + self-driving access.
3
In summer, start at 5 AM. Be on the first shuttle. Finish your big hike by 10:30 AM, then spend the afternoon in the river or at the lodge. Temps exceed 100°F.
4
Day-before Angels Landing lottery works more often than you'd think. Apply 12:01 AM–3 PM the day before. Be flexible with your time slot.
5
Never skip gear rental for The Narrows. ~$25–32 saves your trip. "The rocks are like walking on greased bowling balls." Regular sneakers will not survive.
6
Kolob Canyons is the best-kept secret. Same $35 entry covers all Zion entrances. Same stunning beauty, a fraction of the crowds. 50 min from main canyon via I-15.
7
Observation Point via East Mesa > Angels Landing for views. No permit, no chains, no exposure. Look DOWN on Angels Landing from 2,148ft above the canyon. Arrive by 7 AM for parking.
8
Mid-week visits are dramatically better than weekends for crowds. Avoid Memorial Day and Labor Day weekends entirely.
9
Carry at least 3 liters of water for any major hike. The dry desert air "wicks moisture from your body before you even feel sweaty." Drink before you're thirsty.
10
Download everything before arrival. Cell service is spotty to non-existent on trails. Get offline maps (AllTrails, Google Maps), screenshot permits, and leave your itinerary with someone.
11
Watchman Trail at sunset is an underrated experience. Great views, rarely crowded, and the golden light on the canyon walls is stunning.
12
Zion is a Dark Sky Certified area. Stargazing is exceptional. Pa'rus Trail at night for the Milky Way. Kolob Terrace has minimal light pollution for astrophotography.

Gear Checklist

Always Bring

  • 3+ liters of water per person (park doesn't sell plastic bottles)
  • Reusable water bottle
  • Sturdy hiking boots with ankle support
  • Sunscreen SPF 50+, wide-brim hat, sunglasses
  • Layers — mornings are chilly even when afternoons are hot
  • Backpack with trail snacks (mix, bars, jerky)
  • First aid kit
  • Headlamp / flashlight
  • Paper trail map (from Visitor Center)
  • Offline maps downloaded on phone

Seasonal Additions

  • Summer: Cooling towel, electrolyte packets, extra water
  • Winter: Yaktrax / microspikes ($15–30), warm layers, gloves, beanie
  • Spring/Fall: Rain jacket, extra wind layer

Angels Landing Specific

  • Gloves for chain section (better grip)
  • Nothing dangling — secure all gear
  • No trekking poles on chain section
  • 1 gallon of water per person

Narrows Specific

  • Rent canyoneering boots ($25–32/day)
  • Rent neoprene socks
  • Rent sturdy (non-collapsible) walking stick
  • Dry bag for phone/camera
  • NO cotton — wear merino, polyester, or nylon
  • Extra dry clothes in the car

When to Visit

The Watchman at Sunset
Winter in Zion
Spring (Mar–May)
50–80°F
Wildflowers (mid-April), pleasant hiking temps, waterfalls at peak flow. Narrows often closed April–May due to snowmelt. Crowds build through May. Best for Emerald Pools, Canyon Overlook.
Summer (Jun–Aug)
85–105°F
Extreme heat + largest crowds. Monsoon season mid-July through September (flash flood risk). Start hikes before 8 AM. Narrows usually open in June. Long days.
Fall (Sep–Nov)
BEST OVERALL
"October is the undisputed king of Zion." Mild temps (50s–80s), fall colors peak late Oct, decreasing crowds, excellent Narrows conditions. Best for everything.
Winter (Dec–Feb)
30–50°F
Solitude, snow-dusted cliffs (stunning photos). No shuttle — drive your own car up the canyon. Some trails icy (bring Yaktrax). Narrows too cold. 70% fewer visitors than June.

Hidden Gems

Kolob Canyons
Kolob Terrace
West Rim Trail
Kolob Canyons (NW Section)
Physically separate from main canyon via I-15 north (Exit 40). All the beauty, none of the crowds. 2,000ft cliff walls and deep finger canyons. Taylor Creek Trail and Timber Creek Overlook are highlights. Same $35 entry covers all Zion.
Kolob Terrace Road
Higher elevation = 10–15°F cooler. The Subway trailhead. Northgate Peaks Trail for unique views. Excellent Milky Way photography at night. Starts in Virgin, UT.
East Side / Mt. Carmel Hwy
Underrated zone. Scenic switchbacks, the mile-long tunnel, Checkerboard Mesa. Canyon Overlook trailhead here (no shuttle needed). Many Pools Trail has sculpted sandstone.
Divergent Dam
A "true Zion secret." Crystal-clear water, secluded location ideal for swimming, sunbathing, or picnicking. Ask locally for directions.
Cable Mountain
8 mi RT (from Stave Spring). Remains of a historic cable system used to transport lumber (operated 1901–1926, cables removed 1930). Historical curiosity with great views.
Pine Creek Waterfall
~1 mile off-trail from a dirt pullout on Mt. Carmel Hwy, 1.5 mi north of south entrance. Crystal-clear falls with swimming in summer.

Safety

Flash Floods
The #1 killer in Zion's slot canyons. Can occur with NO warning, even from storms miles away. Check daily flash flood potential at Visitor Center. Monsoon season (mid-Jul–Sep) is highest risk. If water rises or turns muddy: GET TO HIGH GROUND IMMEDIATELY.
Heat
Summer temps regularly exceed 100°F. Drink 1 gallon/day minimum. Start hikes before 8 AM. Heat exhaustion: nausea, dizziness, headache. Heat stroke: confusion, hot/dry skin — call 911.
Falling / Cliffs
Stay on trails and behind barriers. Wet sandstone is extremely slippery. Avoid Angels Landing chain section and Canyon Overlook if wet or icy. Rockfall is common after rain — don't linger under overhangs.
Wildlife
Mule deer and rock squirrels common — do not feed. Rattlesnakes: watch where you step/place hands. Mountain lions present but rarely seen. California condors occasionally spotted.
Swift Water
The Virgin River current can knock you off your feet. Always use a walking stick in the Narrows. Never attempt to cross swiftly flowing water. Face upstream when crossing.
Dehydration
More common than people realize. The dry desert air wicks moisture before you feel sweaty. Drink before you're thirsty. Carry electrolytes. Minimum 3L for any major hike.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

1
Not planning hikes in advance. Know your trailheads, shuttle stops, and timing before you arrive. Cell service won't save you.
2
Arriving mid-morning. Cars wait up to 2 hours to enter the park. Be there before 8 AM or park in Springdale.
3
Underestimating the altitude. The lush scenery is deceptive — trails climb 1,000+ feet at elevation. You'll feel it.
4
Not enough water. 1 quart per 2 hours minimum, 3+ liters for major hikes. Desert air dehydrates you before you realize it.
5
Skipping gear rental for The Narrows. ~$25–32 for boots/socks/stick. Rocks are like "greased bowling balls." Regular sneakers = misery.
6
Hiking Angels Landing at midday. Dangerous congestion on narrow sections with 1,000ft drops. Go early.
7
Allocating only one day. One day = one major hike. Minimum two days recommended for a real experience.
8
Ignoring flash flood warnings. "A clear sky in the canyon doesn't mean it's not pouring miles upstream." If rangers close The Narrows, they mean it.
